## 0. Headline decision (what changed after council)

**Do NOT use "AI Community Day" as the local event's own name.** Both council lenses converged: reusing the name
— even with "local"/"aftershow" appended and a disclaimer — is the single largest risk (implied endorsement +

**Rename the local event → "Aftershow Núcleo IA & GP"**, referencing the PMI global "AI Community Day" only
**descriptively** ("na noite do dia do AI Community Day"), never as the event's title. This sidesteps the
ratification dependency for the save-the-date and removes the brand-collision from the equation.

**Two-track publication (c-level):**

## 1. The risk (why this is a blocker)

The local event shares **date + theme** with the PMI **GLOBAL** "AI Community Day"; the original plan also
shared the **name**. That creates **implied endorsement** — partners/press may assume official PMI sanction or
"AI Standard" umbrella. **PMI-GO is the contracting party (ADR-0104)** and the most exposed: its own board
members personally counter-sign Núcleo certificates, so a misstep lands on named people with skin in the game,
not an abstract "PMI".

Two claim-classes are the live danger:
1. **PDU claim without formal accreditation** — the item most likely to **close Path A**. The Núcleo is not a
   PMI ATP/R.E.P.; it cannot *grant* PDUs. Also a CDC art. 37 (misleading-advertising) exposure to participants.
2. **"Official / parallel / PMI-sanctioned" framing**, or reuse of the global event's name/visual identity.

The genuine, defensible opening: an **aftershow at 19–21h BRT**, a slot the PMI global program (~10–17h BRT)
does not occupy — a real, non-competing complement. Framing must stay inside that truth.

## 7. Council findings (full, for the record)

### legal-counsel — key points
- **Certificate:** use Option A (no PDU). Option B only with PMI's written CCR-category confirmation; **would not
  guess the category** (flagged own recollection as unverified). PDU-without-basis also risks CDC art. 37.
- **Name:** reusing "AI Community Day" as the local title = concorrência desleal / aproveitamento parasitário
  (Lei 9.279/96 art. 195, I/III) + possible trademark infringement if PMI registered it (INPI check pending). →
  use own name, reference descriptively.
- **Contract (ADR-0104):** likely has a PMI brand-use / prior-approval clause — **reread before publishing any
  PMI mention**; this is what most directly exposes PMI-GO to PMI central and can trap Path A.
- **LGPD/Airmeet:** international transfer (arts. 33–36), minimization (Art. 6º III), consent (Art. 7º I),
  **manual Art. 18 channel** (platform cron doesn't reach Airmeet), image consent (#729), sharing-with-PMI as a
  distinct processing; cross-ref #1009 ROPA.

### c-level-advisor — key points
- **Framing:** it's the chapter's community meeting at night, not a PMI extension; the 19–21h slot is the real,
  non-competing axis. Positioning phrase in §3.
- **Two-track publication** (§0): ship the zero-risk save-the-date now; lock name/PDU/certificate until PMI-GO
  answers; if fuse tightens, rename rather than publish unratified.
- **Long-term signal:** a proactive memo (consultation, not after-the-fact notice) treating PMI-GO Presidência as
  a governance partner *reinforces* Path A (demonstrates the brand self-governance PMI Global would need to see);
  a name+PDU incident lands on named PMI-GO board members and becomes a credibility debt (p207).
